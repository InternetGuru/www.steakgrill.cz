Terminology and spelling
Minimize the use of foreign terms and jargon! Before using a foreign term, make sure there is no usable translation. Czech speakers prefer intelligible (standard) Czech; unfamiliar terms tend to put them off.
Always explain foreign terms adequately, ideally by translating or rephrasing them (e.g., broth not bouillon). Alternatively, you can use an interactive glossary, e.g., sous-vide.
Be careful to use foreign terms correctly (e.g.,au jus not Jus).
You can check your spelling using the Internet guide, or the Internet in general.
Consistency
Start each piece of information (each row in the administration box) with a capital letter.
If you are not sure how to write a price or a price range, copy the text from the regular menu and edit it.
Each dish description ends with a period (to maintain consistency). As a result, it should be a sentence with a subject and a verb (not as if the title continues).
Typography
English headings should have capital letters at the start of each word (e.g., Scotch Broth, Pepper Steak). Czech headings only have one at the start (e.g., Hovězí vývar, Tatarský steak).
Distinguish between the en dash (–) and the hyphen (-), and the em dash (—). If the en dash divides two words only, there are no spaces around it (May 1–2). In any other case, use spaces around the dash (April 30 – May 1).
Distinguish between the „Czech“ and “English”quotation marks and the inch sign (").
The apostrophe and the acute accent are different signs, e.g., Fisherman's Chowder (apostrophe) not Fisherman´s Chowder or Fisherman`s Chowder
A space goes between a number and a unit if they are separate words (e.g., one hundred grams (as a noun phrase) = 100 g, whereas one-hundred-gram (as an adjective phrase) = 100g). The same applies to percent and other signs.
The letter case matters, particularly also with units (e.g., g = gram, G = giga-).
Punctuation marks (such as the period, comma, etc.) are followed by a space, with exceptions such as the closing parentheses in this sentence.
A non-breaking space should go between certain words. These are all the words inappropriate to end a line (such as one-letter Czech prepositions) regardless of their place in the text, as they may occur at the end of the line when being printed or in another device.
